My Trip Around The Globe

Mumbai

My visit to Mumbai was filled with a vibrant blend of history and modernity. The city's energy was palpable from the bustling streets of Colaba to the scenic Marine Drive along the Arabian Sea. I enjoyed exploring the Gateway of India and the ancient caves of Elephanta Island, which revealed the depth of Mumbai’s historical roots. I also immersed myself in the local food scene, savoring dishes like vada pav and pav bhaji, which truly embodied the city’s flavor.

Delhi

Delhi, with its rich heritage, was a captivating experience. The city’s monuments, from the Red Fort to the sprawling Qutub Minar complex, showcased India’s imperial past. In Old Delhi, I explored the chaotic yet charming Chandni Chowk, where narrow lanes brimming with shops and food stalls created an unforgettable sensory experience. The blend of history in the walled city and the governmental structures of New Delhi gave me insight into the cultural and political heartbeat of India.

Adid Ababa

In Addis Ababa, I was welcomed by a city that uniquely harmonizes modernity with tradition. The National Museum of Ethiopia housed ancient artifacts, including the famous remains of Lucy, which made me appreciate the country’s role as a cradle of civilization. I also visited the Entoto Hills for panoramic views of the city and experienced the warmth of Ethiopian hospitality through traditional coffee ceremonies and flavorful dishes like injera and doro wat.

Lagos

Lagos offered an exhilarating glimpse into Nigeria's urban life and energy. The city's vibrant art scene, particularly in Lekki and Victoria Island, displayed its evolving culture. I visited the National Museum, which gave insights into Nigeria’s diverse history and traditions. Exploring Lagos’ markets, like Balogun Market, was a lively experience with vendors selling everything from textiles to food, encapsulating the hustle and spirit of the city.
