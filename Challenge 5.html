The zebra is a wild herbivorous mammal known for its unique black-and-white striped coat.

These stripes are distinctive to each individual and may serve purposes like camouflage, confusing predators, social bonding, or even temperature regulation.

Zebras belong to the horse family, Equidae, and are native to Africa.

They are social animals that live in herds, with their main types being the plains zebra, mountain zebra, and Grevy's zebra. Zebras mainly feed on grasses, but they also eat leaves, twigs, and other vegetation.

Their primary predators include lions, hyenas, and crocodiles.
